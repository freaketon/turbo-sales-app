---

## What we're looking for

Dan's newsletter goes out to founders, operators, and content creators in the OUTLIER orbit. The best submissions share three traits:

1. **A specific, repeatable insight** — a framework, teardown, or counter-intuitive observation the reader can act on the same day.
2. **A real story behind it** — a customer call, a launch, a number, a screenshot. Not a generic "5 tips" listicle.
3. **A voice that sounds like Dan** — direct, opinionated, built-in-public, founder-to-founder. No corporate hedging, no AI sludge.

If a concept could be published verbatim by any SaaS blog, it's not for us.

---

## The bar (rubric the Media team uses)

Each concept is scored 1–5 on:

| Criterion          | What "5" looks like                                                                       |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Hook**           | First line makes the reader stop scrolling. Specific number, named tension, or POV claim. |
| **Insight**        | Reader walks away with one thing they didn't know or hadn't framed that way.              |
| **Proof**          | A real example, customer quote, screenshot, or data point — not hypotheticals.            |
| **Actionability**  | A reader can do something with it tomorrow.                                               |
| **Voice fit**      | Sounds like Dan wrote it, not a content marketer.                                         |

Anything averaging <3.5 gets passed on. Two 5s on Hook + Insight can carry a submission even if Proof is thin.

## Format the Media team expects per concept

Each submission produces **one outline** with this shape:

```
TITLE / WORKING SUBJECT LINE
HOOK (the first 1–2 sentences of the email)
THESIS (one sentence — the claim or the insight)
WHY THIS, WHY NOW (the trigger — what made you think of this)
BODY OUTLINE (3–6 bullets — sections / beats / arguments)
PROOF (story, screenshot, quote, number — what makes it real)
CTA (what the reader does next — reply / share / book / try X)
NOTES FOR EDITOR (anything Dan should know — sensitive names, do-not-publish-until, etc.)
```

The SKILL.md in this folder walks you through the questions and produces this outline for you.

## Topic territory (use as inspiration, not a checklist)

- Sales / discovery call frameworks (TURBO → OUTLIER, the 12-section script, objection handling)
- Content intelligence — what's working in short-form, trend lifecycle, competitor monitoring
- Founder-to-founder lessons — pricing, the Founders Circle/Early Adopter offer, beta launches
- Building in public — engineering decisions, rewrites, things that broke
- Customer wins / teardowns (with permission)
- Contrarian takes on widely-repeated content/sales advice

## What won't make it

- Recycled LinkedIn-bait ("Here are 10 things every founder should know…")
- "Why AI changes everything" without a specific lens
- Pure product announcements with no story
- Anything you couldn't defend in a comment thread

---

## After you submit

The Media team triages within ~5 business days. Approved concepts get drafted by the writing team — you'll be looped in for a fact-check pass before publish. No guarantee any specific idea is used; the bar is high on purpose.
