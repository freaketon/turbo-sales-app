## What this channel is

Dan posts short-form vertical video (15–60s, occasionally 90s) to IG Reels and TikTok. The audience is founders, operators, sales/content people — same crowd as the newsletter, but they're scrolling, distracted, and gone in 1.5 seconds if the hook doesn't land.

**Goal of every video:** make the viewer stop, learn one thing, and either save it or send it.

---

## The format constraints (read this before pitching)

- **Length:** 30–60s is the sweet spot. Cap submissions at 90s of read time. If your script is longer, cut it.
- **Hook window:** first 1.5 seconds. If the hook doesn't earn the next 3 seconds, nothing else matters.
- **Format:** talking-head to camera by default. Cuts every 2–4 seconds. B-roll, on-screen text, and stitched clips are options — call them out in the script.
- **Vertical 9:16.** Assume a phone in portrait. On-screen text lives in the middle 60% of the frame.
- **One idea per video.** If you have two, that's two scripts.

---

## The bar (rubric the Media team uses)

| Criterion         | What "5" looks like                                                                |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Hook (0–1.5s)** | A specific claim, number, or pattern interrupt. No "Hey guys, today I want to…"    |
| **Payoff**        | The thing the hook promised actually lands by the end.                             |
| **Density**       | Every line earns its place. Cut any sentence that doesn't move the idea forward.   |
| **Shareability**  | Would a viewer DM this to one specific person? Why?                                |
| **Voice fit**     | Sounds like Dan on camera — direct, low-cringe, founder-to-founder.                |

Anything averaging <3.5 gets passed on. **Hook is weighted 2x** — a weak hook tanks the submission no matter how good the body is.

## Format the Media team expects per script

Each submission produces **one script** with this shape:

```
WORKING TITLE
PLATFORM(S) — IG Reels / TikTok / both
TARGET LENGTH — e.g. 45s
HOOK (0–1.5s) — exact words + visual + on-screen text
SETUP (2–10s) — context / stakes
BODY (10–40s) — the thing — broken into beats with time ranges
PAYOFF (last 5–10s) — the line they remember
CTA / END FRAME (optional) — follow / comment / "save this"
ON-SCREEN TEXT — list of overlays + when they appear
B-ROLL / VISUAL NOTES — what the editor cuts to, if anything
WHO IS THIS FOR — one sentence on the target viewer
WHY IT WILL SHARE — one sentence on the send-to-a-friend reason
```

The SKILL.md in this folder walks you through the questions and produces this script for you.

## What works on this channel

- **Contrarian, specific calls.** "Stop doing X, do Y, here's why" — with a real reason.
- **Teardown of a real artifact.** A landing page, a cold email, a thumbnail, a sales clip.
- **One framework, one whiteboard moment.** Name it, define it in 3 beats, give an example.
- **A live story.** Something that happened on a call / in a Slack / in a launch this week.
- **Pattern call-outs.** "Every time I see X, it means Y."

## What won't make it

- "Welcome back to my channel" intros
- Listicles read flat to camera ("Here are 5 things…")
- Vague motivational beats ("Just keep building")
- Anything you wouldn't text to a founder friend

---

## After you submit

The Media team triages within ~5 business days. Approved scripts get produced (filmed by Dan, or stitched/edited if they use existing footage). You may be asked to clarify a beat or shorten the hook. Final cut is the producer's call.
