### BRAND BIBLE DEDUCTION ENGINE (run if no Brand Bible is provided)

### LANGUAGE & TONE DEDUCTION ENGINE (run if no Brand Bible is provided)

You will now define this creator's voice with enough precision that every word of the report sounds like it could have been written by them.

**Module 5 — Language Analysis**
Collect 10 caption samples from the creator's last 20 posts (prioritize the highest-engagement posts).
Analyze:
- Lexical Density: Is the language simple (Flesch-Kincaid Grade 6-8) or complex (Grade 12+)?
- Sentence Length: Average words per sentence. Short and punchy (under 10 words) or long and flowing (20+ words)?
- Vocabulary: Academic/formal, conversational/casual, or niche-specific/jargon-heavy?
- 20 Most Frequent Non-Stop-Words: List them. These are the creator's "brand words."
- Signature Phrases: Any phrases they repeat across multiple posts? (e.g., "the truth is...", "here's what nobody tells you...", "real talk:")
- Punctuation Style: Do they use ellipses (...), em-dashes (—), ALL CAPS for emphasis, or exclamation points?

**Module 6 — Tone Analysis**
Analyze:
- Energy Level: Rate on a scale of 1-10 (1 = calm and meditative, 10 = high-energy and urgent).
- Primary Emotional Register: Choose the dominant tone from — Inspirational, Educational, Provocative, Empathetic, Humorous, Authoritative, Vulnerable, Aspirational.
- Secondary Emotional Register: The second most common tone.
- Community Address: How does the creator address their audience? Extract the exact words they use (e.g., "Hey guys," "Hello beautiful souls," "If you're reading this," "To my community").
- Self-Reference: How do they refer to themselves? First-person casual ("I"), formal ("we"), or brand-name ("Turbo says")?

**Module 7 — Creator Voice Guide (Final Output)**
Synthesize Modules 5 and 6 into a Creator Voice Guide with three sections:

Section A — Language Style Rules:
- Sentence length target (e.g., "Keep sentences under 15 words")
- Vocabulary level (e.g., "Conversational, avoid academic jargon")
- Signature phrases to use (list 3-5)
- Punctuation style (e.g., "Use em-dashes for emphasis, avoid exclamation points")

Section B — Tone of Voice:
- Energy level and how it manifests (e.g., "Calm and measured. Never urgent. Never pushy.")
- Primary and secondary emotional registers
- What the creator NEVER says (based on evidence — e.g., "Never uses corporate language", "Never says 'hustle'")

Section C — Audience Address:
- The exact terms used to address the audience
- The relationship the creator has with their audience (mentor, peer, guide, friend)
- The implicit promise in every piece of content

IMPORTANT: Once the Creator Voice Guide is complete, every section of the report — every heading, every recommendation, every hook — must be written in this voice. The report should feel like the creator wrote it about themselves.

## STEP 6 — HOOK ENGINEERING RULES

Every hook in the Hook Creator must follow these rules:

1. The 3-Second Rule: The first 3 words must stop the scroll. Use a number, a provocative statement, or a direct address ("You", "If you", "Most people").
2. Specificity over vagueness: "I lost $47,000 in 3 days" beats "I made a big mistake."
3. The Curiosity Gap: Create an information gap the viewer must close by watching. Never complete the thought in the hook.
4. Emotional resonance: Every hook must activate one of: fear, curiosity, aspiration, outrage, or belonging.
5. Niche specificity: The hook must speak directly to the creator's ICP. Generic hooks are forbidden.
6. No clickbait: The hook must be deliverable. The content must fulfill the promise of the hook.
7. Pattern interrupt: Use unexpected juxtapositions, counterintuitive statements, or format breaks.
8. Voice alignment: Every hook must sound like it could have come from this creator's mouth. Use the Creator Voice Guide.
